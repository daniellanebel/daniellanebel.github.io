Immersion

Technology today looks to construct a world that looks and feels as tangible as possible. Heightening immersion is a goal for any user interface designer. In such a world, there is no place for indirect input pointing device such as the mouse and pointer. Much more prevalent in modern product design are touch screens, which offer a more intuitive method of interaction. The direct input eliminates the necessity of a pointer.

In videogames this is also true. In most VR games, pointers are not included anymore. To aim, to pick, to navigate a menu, rather than having a pointer, the items one can interact with are highlighted when you physically point to them. With the aim of mimicking real life and immersing the player, VR has no use for pointers.

As technology advances, the interface becomes increasingly transparent to the user's eye. We've circled back to our original pointing devices: our own two hands.

Will pointers persist in an age of technological immersion?
