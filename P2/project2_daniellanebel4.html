Mac Pointers Through Time:

The first mouse, alongside the first mouse cursor, were invented in 1970 by Douglas Engelbart. These were used for the first time with the Xerox Alto Computer System in 1973.

The original cursor was designed pointing straight up; the slanted version we have today was implemented though, since it was easier for low resolution displays to show plus a diagonal cursor was easier to keep track of on screen.

Simple and straightforward, for many years the default pointer has served its purpose without much change to its design; although many variations have been implemented to facilitate the user's interaction with several functions of the software. These special cursors appear when the pointer has the ability to do more than just pointing. When given the ability to select and highlight text for example, the cursor will turn into a horizontal line that blinks. Pictured at the top are a number of examples of the Mac Os' pointer variations.

The default pointer can change if it is given a custom design. Customizable pointers, possible on websites, became popular in the 90s, when people designed perzonalized blogs. People could design the pointer to look like anything; animated cursors were also possible. The variety is endless.
